One evening I installed Claude Code, pointed it at a rough mockup, and asked it to build a frontend. What came out looked like a talented designer had spent weeks on it. I basically just watched.

I'd been avoiding this project for almost a year. Life, work, the usual. But something about this was different.

I started wiring up backend services. Before I knew it, it was 3am and I had a functioning app. Over the next four evenings I added billing, a news feed, market data integrations. Built three MCP tools. Twenty hours total.

But the high wasn't from the speed.

I started using AI as a sparring partner too, making Gemini fight me on my auth design, running proposals through multiple models to find flaws. That used to require finding a senior engineer with the right expertise and the willingness to argue with you at midnight.

That's when it clicked. I wasn't missing the coding. I was missing the building.

Building is the architecture, the tradeoffs, the decisions that actually matter. Coding is the drafting. AI handles that now. What's left is the part I actually love.

I'm shipping things again.
